The asymmetry that decides everything
Being roughly right about the spread costs efficiency. Being wrong about the structure of the errors costs validity. The iid likelihood commits to a false structure, so its interval is both too narrow and too rarely right.
Act VI · Honest uncertainty — the mechanism

n, n², n³: what an error's shape does to a sum

\[ e_t = \omega + \varepsilon_t, \qquad \mathrm{Var}\!\Big(\sum_{t=1}^{n} e_t\Big) = n^2\,\sigma_\omega^2 + n\,\sigma_\varepsilon^2 \] one persistent offset ω per world, held for the whole window, on top of fresh weekly noise; the offset term compounds as n², not n
  • Independent errors: the sum's variance grows as \(n\). That is the iid bet.
  • Constant offset: \(n^2\). Its autocorrelation is flat: a level shift is equally present at every lag, exactly the ACF the placebo worlds measured.
  • Near-integrated random walk: \(n^3\). The truth here sits between \(n^2\) and \(n^3\).

AR(1) matches none of these shapes: its ACF decays geometrically, the decay the data refused. What it prices is the sum: ρ is pulled up until the summed variance comes out right at this window length.

When the shortcut stops working
AR(1) lands the total's scale at \(n = {{nb07.n_post}}\) weeks. Much longer windows force the honest route: a random-walk or local-level state-space component that matches the shape itself, not just this one sum.

Act VII · What can go wrong — spillover

The violation no placebo can see

If the campaign leaks into neighbouring donor markets, every placebo refit is contaminated in exactly the same way. Nothing looks unusual. Only a simulator can plant the violation and watch.
\[ \text{bias} \;\approx\; -\,\varphi \sum_{j \in \text{nbrs}} w_j \,\times\, \tau \] leaked lift raises the contaminated donors, so the synthetic rises and the measured gap shrinks by roughly the spilled weight

Planted in the simulator: the formula predicts a bias of {{nb07.sp_predicted}} €k; the refit measures {{nb07.sp_measured}} €k. The mechanism is understood, not just observed.

Fix by design, not statistics
Spillover attenuates: it understates a real campaign, never inflates it. That is conservative for a GO, though it can turn a true GO into a NO-GO. Since no placebo can flag it, drop adjacent and media-overlapping markets from the donor pool before fitting anything. A smaller clean pool beats a larger contaminated one.

A real advertiser, and a referee money can't argue with

Randomization licenses an estimator that fits no model at all: each pair's treated-minus-control change through launch, averaged. Unbiased by construction: the closest thing to truth real data ever hands anyone.

  • Pairing is variance reduction: it differences geo size out within each pair, cutting the SE {{nb07b.pairing_gain}}×, from ${{nb07b.unpaired_se}}k to ${{nb07b.did_se}}k.
Unbiasedness is not precision
Even paired, the anchor's interval spans nearly half the effect in each direction. That width is the entire commercial reason to fit a model on top of a perfectly good experiment.
